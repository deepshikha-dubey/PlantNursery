A plant nursery is a facility where plants are grown, cultivated, and sold for various purposes. These nurseries play a crucial role in the propagation and care of plants until they reach a suitable age for transplantation. Here are some key points about plant nurseries: Responsibilities of Nursery Workers: Plant Care: Nursery workers nurture growing shrubs, trees, and flowers. Propagation: They select seeds, prepare soil, and propagate plants. Maintenance: Watering, trimming, and repotting are part of their daily tasks. Customer Interaction: Nursery workers provide advice to clients on plant selection and care. Types of Plants Grown: Flowers: Nurseries cultivate flowering plants like roses, lilies, and daisies. Trees: Saplings and young trees are raised until they’re ready for planting. Shrubs: These include ornamental shrubs and hedge plants. Education and Requirements: A high school diploma is often the minimum requirement. An associate degree in agriculture or horticulture can be beneficial. Some positions may require a bachelor’s degree in horticulture or related fields..
